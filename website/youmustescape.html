Dear “Uncle” McAllister,


By now, you’ve noticed the cold tile against your skin and the stale, metallic
taste in your mouth. Welcome to your new home. Fitting, really. You always
struck me as someone who tried to keep things clean. Let's see how tidy your
mind stays under pressure.

You’ve been so very difficult to get alone, Mr. McAllister. Always locked doors,
always lights on, always someone watching. But not anymore. Now it’s just
you, me, and the quiet drip of that leaky pipe you’ll learn to hate. I’ve left you
some clues, little breadcrumbs to earn your freedom. Think of it as a
game—one you must win.

If you solve the puzzle, you get to walk out. If not, well… I suppose the flies
will find you eventually. They always do. Try not to scream. The walls are
thick, and the only ears that listen in here are mine. You have until the lights
go out again. After that, it’s just the dark—and me.

Looking forward to seeing how smart you really are,

The Personalia
